Mike Stevenson

Recently I had the pleasure of meeting Mike in a dueling piano show at the Uptown Social House and as a solo artist at Pepperwood Bistro in Burlington. This will be his first appearance at Alapalooza. Mike Stevenson initially developed his repertoire in Western Europe's smaller venues; gaining valuable experience before returning to Canada to join the Thinline R&B revue.  The band became Clarksdale with Sam Carothers (The Allman Brothers, Ronnie Hawkins), Rob Quail (Jeff Healey Band), and Nelson Poirier and - while reviewing it - John Grant of TO Nite wrote "Mike Stevenson's devastating barrel house left hand and high register work is truly captivating".  Mike now tours internationally with ABBA Revisited and plays in 80's trio Spandau Cafe as well as The Yachty Rockers.  He also enjoys playing piano bars and dueling piano shows as well as festivals and private events.  Mike is extremely proud of the Canadian music scene and is grateful to all of its supporters. Mike will be accompanied by Stan Octabienski on drums.
Mike Stevenson Facebook page

Lou Moore

Guitar virtuoso and country/folk artist extraordinaire Lou Moore makes his debut at Alapalooza. Lou is from Richmond Hill and is a longtime cottager on Raven Lake. He is well known in the Dorset area, having played many Tall Pines Snowmobile Club and other community events. Lou Moore has played thousands of engagements over his 50 year history. He was a member of the Good Brothers Band for close to 5 years in the 1980’s and played with The Tommy Hunter touring band for a short time. Lou has also played with hundreds of local players in the genre of old time rock ‘n roll, country rock, traditional country and blues. There are several youtube videos of his different bands that featured musicians like Jimmy Bowskill on mandolin (The Sheepdogs, Blue Rodeo), Wendell Ferguson (CCMA guitar player of the year and CCMA hall of fame member), Roly Platt (harmonica for Ronnie Hawkins band, Matt Minglewood) and several bass players of renown. Lou remains one of the finest acoustic guitar players in Canada and knows hundreds of songs. Lou will be joined by the always fantastic Michael McMaster on bass and backup vocals.

VH TRIO / NEIL YOUNG LEGACY

Gerald Van Halteren (GVH) will be performing with his band VH Trio at Alapalooza, playing the best of classic rock from the 60’s and 70’s. He will also perform a set of classics from Canadian legend Neil Young. Gerald has been performing live music since the late 1970s, playing guitar, bass, and providing vocals. After moving from Lindsay to Toronto in the early '80s, he formed a band called Praying Mantis, which played original songs and hard rock covers. The band had the opportunity to open for acts like Kim Mitchell and Teenage Head. Upon returning to Lindsay, he joined a pop-rock band called Tinted Vision, opening for touring artists such as Glass Tiger, the Blushing Brides, and others. Following his time on the road, Gerald formed a three-piece classic rock band called Red Line, once again opening for Kim Mitchell and Teenage Head, as well as Blue Öyster Cult and Edgar Winter. He shared the stage with iconic Canadian bands such as April Wine and Rick Emmett (of Triumph) at the Great Canadian Rock and Roll Festival. He also opened for Goddo, then walked off stage only to return and perform as part of Goddo with Greg. One night, while playing a solo set at the Grand Hotel in Lindsay, friends showed up with Jeff Healey in tow. Jeff got up and joined Gerald for the second set. Way too cool! In the early 1990s, Gerald formed a Neil Young tribute band called Only Young, touring extensively throughout Ontario and Quebec until the early 2000s. Later, he teamed up with Rich Howard (Filthy Rich & the Empty Pockets) to form Plan 9, performing in both Lindsay and Toronto. In the late 2000s, Gerald began performing with his sons, Gerrit and Caleb, forming The VH Trio, a group that continues to perform to this day. Gerald has hosted countless jam nights since the 1980s, had his own TV show on Cable 10, and performed in the orchestra pit for several musical stage productions at the Academy Theatre in Lindsay. Most recently, he started a new Neil Young tribute project called Legacy, featuring Rich Howard on bass and harmonica, Sean Cotton on slide guitar, and John Kidd on drums. He may have missed a few juicy details, but it’s been one hell of a rock 'n' roll ride—and he’s looking forward to meeting and playing for all of you.

GALACTIC REDEMPTION

Joining us for the first time at Alapalooza is Galactic Redemption, a local reggae band from the Huntsville area. Galactic redemption is a band raised in Muskoka, featuring Jeremy Waterhouse on guitar and vocals and Jamaican percussionist Everton “Pablo” Paul on drums. As a powerful six piece band, they will be joined by their bandmates on bass, keyboards, sax and horns. Deep in their local music scene for over 20 years, with roots in rock reggae blues sounds, they’re always stoked to play and get the party going!

TONE DEAF

Back for their 2 nd year at Alapalooza, this year they will be our headliner. As a matter of a quick introduction, Tone Deaf are a classic rock band that focuses primarily on 70's rock… but better known as “A drinking band with a rock and roll problem!” Maybe not the prettiest band going (however, we’ve seen tons uglier), nor the most talented (they are impressed when we hold the guitar the right way), and maybe not the “hardest working band”, but they can probably drink all those bands under the table! That’s right, they are a party band! It’s rock and roll… this ain’t rocket science. You drink, you dance, you have a great time, you hate yourself in the morning. Tone Deaf started back in the year 2000 as a way to get out of the house and have a couple of pints with the fellas. Not much has really changed since then. Well, the band has changed a bit… people come and people go… but the main focus of the band remains the same. Have a cocktail and have a good time! Ian Nielsen has played bass guitar at Alapalooza many times with Tone Deaf, Gap 33 and The Four Fingers and was one of our sound guys for the past two years. Along with Ian Neilsen (bass) are Marianne Mazur (lead singer, guitar), Stan Octabienski (drums, vocals) and Mark Van Overdijk (guitar, vocals).
