Fiddlestix

Back for their second year at Alapalooza, Fiddlestix is ready to deliver another highenergy show! This is a show you don’t want to miss! Steve Bowen, Yamaha endorsed electric fiddler, and his award winning team blend the energy of a Rock Concert with Celtic, Country and Folk music creating what has been dubbed 'FIDDLE-ROCK' by Y108 Rocks Hamilton. With Bowen's unparalleled violin skills, Steve and the band are defining Fiddlestix as one of the top Canadian bands due to their signature sound, instrumental layering, multiple lead vocal trade-offs, and incredible four-part harmonies, flexing genres and blending styles seamlessly.   Fiddlestix recently won the Milton Reader’s Choice 2023 Platinum Award for best local band and have won numerous local awards since 2019. Fiddlestix has shared the stage with some of Canada's finest musicians, including Big Sugar, Leahy, Gord Downie, Great Big Sea, Jim Cuddy, The Irish Rovers, Honeymoon Suite and many more. Fiddlestix headlined the CNE East Coast Kitchen Party 2018 and 2019 and performed an astonishing run of 36 shows over 5 days for the Celtic Festival at the CNE in 2022. Joining Steve Bowen (electric violin, vocals) are Nancy Cipollone (guitar, vocals), Matthew Bowker (bass, vocals), Scott Apted (drums, vocals), and Jeff Blake (keys, vocals).

TONE DEAF

You may recognize Ian Nielsen from previous years at Alapalooza. He has filled in on bass guitar for Gap 33 and The Four Fingers and was one of our sound guys in 2023. This year he will be playing bass with his regular Burlington based band Tone Deaf. As a matter of a quick introduction, Tone Deaf are a classic rock band that focuses primarily on 70's rock… but better known as “A drinking band with a rock and roll problem!” Maybe not the prettiest band going (however, we’ve seen tons uglier), nor the most talented (they are impressed when we hold the guitar the right way), and maybe not the “hardest working band”, but they can probably drink all those bands under the table! That’s right, they are a party band! It’s rock and roll… this ain’t rocket science. You drink, you dance, you have a great time, you hate yourself in the morning. Tone Deaf started back in the year 2000 as a way to get out of the house and have a couple of pints with the fellas. Not much has really changed since then. Well, the band has changed a bit… people come and people go… but the main focus of the band remains the same. Have a cocktail and have a good time! Along with Ian Neilsen (bass) are Marianne Mazur (lead singer, guitar), Stan Octabienski (drums, vocals) and Mark Van Overdijk (guitar, vocals).

The Duomatics

The Duomatics are back for their 3 rd performance at Alapalooza, this year a father/son duo. The Duomatics are a Montreal area acoustic duo, specializing in great rock and pop songs from the 60's to today. Multi-instrumentalist and vocalists, Peter Bowering (vocals, guitar, drums) and his son Luke Bowering (drums, vocals, guitar) literally switch it up during the show, switching instruments for more variety and entertainment value. Luke may throw in a few 50’s tunes and a couple originals to showcase his voice and talents.
The Duomatics's Facebook Page

Steve Wallace

Steve is a fellow Porcupine Bay cottager and performer from down the bay. He has played for us at Alapalooza since our 2 nd year, making this year #9. Steve has performed with his son Dylan in Gap 33 (2016 to 2019), with the Dylan Wallace Band (2020, 2021, 2023) and with Compliments of the House (2022). For Alapalooza 2024, Steve will be dazzling us with a solo performance on keyboard and vocals. I’m sure you will be singing along to some classics!

Michelle Hedley

You will no doubt recognize Michelle, as she has played for us previously at Alapalooza with The Mighty Fish band (2019) and The Mighty Fish duo with Kit Allen (2021, 2022). Michelle is a local Grimsby musician who has developed a very successful solo career playing wineries and pubs in the Niagara Region as well as Burlington. She plays all your favorite covers, blending folk, rock, pop and country with her own twist!
